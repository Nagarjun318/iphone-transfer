How It Works

1

Install iTunes (first time only)

iTunes provides the USB drivers Apple requires for cable communication. You can uninstall iTunes afterward â€” the drivers (usbmuxd.exe) remain.

2

Download and run Setup

Click "Download Setup" above. Run iPhoneTransfer-Setup.exe. Windows will install the app to your user profile â€” no admin rights needed.

3

Connect iPhone via USB cable

Use the original Apple cable or a certified MFi cable. Plug into a USB-A or USB-C port directly on your laptop â€” avoid hubs for best speed.

4

Unlock and "Trust This Computer"

Unlock your iPhone with your passcode. When the Trust This Computer? dialog appears, tap Trust and enter your passcode. This happens once per PC.

5

Scan and select photos

Click Scan Photos. Your camera roll appears as a grid of thumbnails. Select individual files or click Select All.

6

Choose folder and transfer

Click Browse to pick a Windows folder. Then click Transfer Selected Files. Progress shows per-file speed and estimated time remaining.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to keep iTunes installed?

No. iTunes is only needed for its USB drivers (Apple Mobile Device Support). After installing iTunes once, you can uninstall the iTunes app and the drivers remain. The drivers are a small background service â€” not the full iTunes UI.

Does this work with iPad?

Yes! iPad uses the same AFC protocol as iPhone. The app will detect your iPad automatically.

Will my photos lose quality?

Never. Files are transferred byte-for-byte â€” the exact same file that's on your iPhone arrives on your PC. HEIC, HEIF, MOV, MP4 â€” all preserved without any conversion.

My iPhone shows "Trust This Computer?" every time. Why?

This usually means the pairing record was deleted â€” either you reset your iPhone, or tapped "Forget This Computer" in Settings. Simply tap Trust again. You shouldn't need to do this more than once per PC per iPhone-reset.

How does auto-update work?

ClickOnce checks for a new version on this website every 7 days when you launch the app. If a newer version is found, it downloads automatically in the background and applies on the next launch. No action needed from you.

Can I delete photos from iPhone with this app?

No â€” by design. The app has read-only access to your camera roll (Apple's AFC service doesn't allow deleting from DCIM). Delete photos using the iPhone's own Photos app after transfer.
